# REZ — Complete Business Model (2026-04-21)

## Executive Summary

REZ is building India's universal QR transaction layer — a habit-forming platform that turns every payment into an earning opportunity. The platform operates a net-zero coin economy (1 coin = 1 INR earned and spent) that creates powerful retention mechanics without generating payment liability. REZ serves four distinct customer segments simultaneously: consumers who earn coins on every transaction, merchants who acquire customers and reduce payment processing costs, hotels that gain a direct booking channel with integrated loyalty, and advertisers who reach a captured audience at the moment of purchase intent. The company generates revenue through transaction fees, coin economy margins, advertising, and subscription tiers. The core flywheel is: Scan -> Pay -> Earn Coins -> Discover -> Repeat — each transaction compounds engagement and creates cross-selling opportunities.

## Problem Statement

### Consumer Side
- Retail loyalty programs are fragmented across brands with no interoperability
- Cashback and rewards are either too small to matter or too complex to redeem
- No single platform connects in-store shopping, dining, hotels, and travel
- Digital payments provide no incremental value beyond convenience
- Small merchants offer no loyalty program at all

### Merchant Side
- Payment processing fees eat 2-3% of every transaction (razorpay/UPI cost)
- Customer acquisition costs are high with no repeat-visit guarantee
- Aggregators (Swiggy/Zomato) take 20-30% commission and own the customer relationship
- Point-of-sale systems are expensive, fragmented, and require multiple vendors
- No unified analytics across in-store, online, and delivery channels

### Hotel Side
- OTAs (MakeMyTrip, Goibibo) charge 15-25% commission per booking
- Direct booking websites have zero traction without brand recognition
- PMS systems are expensive and require dedicated staff
- No loyalty program that spans across restaurant, stay, and retail

### Advertiser Side
- Meta/Google advertising has high CPMs and low purchase intent targeting
- Small merchants cannot afford to advertise on mainstream platforms
- No closed-loop attribution from ad impression to coin redemption to purchase

## Value Proposition

- **Real Value Redemption**: Coins redeemable at any participating merchant — no complicated points systems
- **Social Discovery**: Find nearby stores, see ratings, get personalized recommendations
- **Habit Formation**: The coin loop creates a mental accounting benefit that drives repeat visits

### For Merchants

- **Zero-Cost Customer Acquisition**: QR codes bring customers in without paying for advertising
- **Keep Your Customers**: Unlike aggregators, merchants own the customer relationship through the REZ platform
- **Lower Payment Costs**: UPI/QR payments cost 0% under certain merchant tiers (vs. 2-3% for cards)
- **All-in-One POS**: No need for separate POS, loyalty, and analytics systems — REZ provides one dashboard

### For Hotels

- **Lower Commission**: Direct booking channel at 5-10% commission vs. 15-25% from OTAs

- **Coin Loyalty**: Hotel guests earn and redeem REZ coins across the entire ecosystem

### For Advertisers

- **Purchase-Intent Audience**: REZ users are actively transacting, not just browsing
- **Closed-Loop Attribution**: Every ad impression can be tracked to QR scan to coin redemption to purchase
- **Local Targeting**: Reach users within 1-5km of a store location

- **Affordable CPM**: Local merchants can advertise for Rs 50-500/day vs. Rs 5000+ on Meta

### 2. Coin Economy Margin

**The Net-Zero Model**
- Consumers earn 1 coin per 1 INR spent
- Consumers redeem 1 coin = 1 INR at any merchant
- REZ takes a small spread on coin transactions

**Spread Mechanics**
- Coin purchase from REZ: 1 coin = 1 INR + 0% spread
- Coin redemption by merchant: merchant receives 0.99 INR per coin redeemed
- REZ margin: 1% of every redeemed coin (absorbed in the ecosystem)

**Branded Coin Revenue**
- Merchants can issue branded coins with custom denominations
- REZ charges 2-5% issuance fee for branded coins
- Example: A salon chain issues 10,000 branded coins at Rs 0.90 each = Rs 9,000 to REZ

**Coin Expiry Revenue**
- Coins with configurable expiry (30/60/90 days) that expire unclaimed
- Estimated 5-10% of coins expire unredeemed
- Expired coins are not refunded — this is disclosed to users

**TRY Trials**
- Merchants pay a commitment fee (Rs 9/19/29) to offer free trials
- REZ takes 15% of commitment fees

## Unit Economics

### Consumer Side

**Acquisition Cost (CAC)**
- Organic: Rs 0 (word of mouth, QR scanning)
- Paid referral: Rs 50 per referred user who makes first transaction
- Paid advertising: Rs 100-200 per acquired user (via AdBazaar cross-promotion)
- Average blended CAC: Rs 30-50

**Lifetime Value (LTV)**
- Average transaction value: Rs 200
- Transactions per month: 4 (once per week)
- Monthly GMV per user: Rs 800
- Average user tenure: 18 months
- LTV: Rs 14,400 GMV
- Platform take rate (avg): 1.5%
- Gross LTV: Rs 216
- COGS (SMS, push, server): Rs 5
- Gross Margin LTV: Rs 211
- Blended CAC: Rs 40
- LTV/CAC ratio: 5.3x

**Coin Loop Economics**
- User earns 1 coin per 1 INR spent
- User redeems coins at taking merchants
- Net coin flow: slightly more coins earned than redeemed (5-10% expire)
- Coin expiry benefit: ~Rs 2-5 per user per year

### Coin Economics (Earn/Spend Model)

**Earning**
- Consumer spends Rs 100 -> earns 100 coins
- REZ cost: Rs 0 (coins are a liability, not a cost)

**Spending**
- Consumer redeems 100 coins at merchant
- Merchant receives Rs 99 (REZ takes Rs 1 = 1% platform fee)
- REZ revenue: Rs 1 per redemption

**Expiry**
- Estimated 7% of coins expire unclaimed
- This is pure profit for REZ (no fulfillment cost)
- At 1 crore transactions/year: ~Rs 70 lakhs in expiry revenue

**Branded Coins**
- Merchant buys 1,00,000 coins at Rs 0.90 each = Rs 90,000
- REZ revenue: Rs 10,000 (11% margin)
- Coins given to consumers at 1 coin = 1 INR
- Merchant redeems at Rs 0.99 per coin
- Net cost to merchant: Rs 0.91 per coin delivered to consumer

**TRY Trials**
- Merchant sets up trial: Rs 9-29 commitment fee
- Consumer gets free trial product/service
- REZ takes 15%: Rs 1.35-4.35 per trial
- Consumer pays REZ fee, merchant provides free trial
- REZ earns on the transaction even for free trials

### Differentiation

1. **Cross-Vertical Loyalty**: Earn coins at a restaurant, redeem at a hotel, spend at a salon — no other platform does this
2. **Scan-to-Earn**: QR scanning is the simplest possible interaction — no app download friction at merchant locations
3. **Merchant-First**: Unlike aggregators, REZ does not compete with merchants for customers — merchants own the relationship
4. **AdBazaar**: Small merchants can advertise affordably with closed-loop attribution — never before possible
5. **Habit Loop**: The coin earning/spending cycle creates genuine habitual behavior

### Moat

1. **Network Effects**: More merchants -> more QR codes -> more coins to earn -> more consumers -> more merchant value
2. **Data Moat**: Transaction history across food, retail, salon, hotel creates unmatched consumer insights
3. **Habit Moat**: Once consumers are earning coins, they scan every QR code they see — switching costs are psychological
4. **Merchant Lock-In**: Switching POS systems is painful; merchants invested in REZ will stay
5. **AdBazaar Moat**: Advertisers cannot get purchase-intent attribution on any other platform for local merchants

## Growth Engine

### Network Effects

**Consumer-Merchant Flywheel**
```
More Merchants -> More QR codes -> More coin earning opportunities
     ^                                    |
     |                                    v
More Consumer                    More coin redemptions
Engagement                      -> More merchant value
     ^                                    |
     |____________________________________|
```

**Every QR scan creates a double-sided network effect**

### Flywheel Mechanics

1. **Consumer Acquisition**: QR codes at merchant locations bring in new users for free (organic)
2. **Coin Incentive**: New users earn coins immediately on first scan, creating instant value
3. **Discovery**: Users discover new merchants through the app to earn more coins
4. **Redemption**: Users spend coins at their favorite merchants, creating loyalty
5. **Referral**: Happy users refer friends (Rs 50 worth of coins per referral)
6. **Merchant Growth**: More merchants join to reach existing coin-earning consumers
7. **AdBazaar**: Merchants advertise on REZ to reach consumers already in the ecosystem

### Virality

- **QR Codes**: Every merchant QR is a free distribution channel
- **Referral**: Users earn Rs 50 per successful referral
- **Social Sharing**: Users share deals and coin earnings on WhatsApp
- **Merchant Co-Marketing**: Merchants promote their REZ presence
- **TRY Trials**: Free trials spread by word of mouth

## Risk Factors

### Regulatory

- **UPI/NPCI Regulations**: Changes to UPI fee structures could impact merchant economics
- **Payment Aggregator License**: REZ may need a PA license from RBI (currently uses Razorpay)
- **Data Privacy (DPDP Act)**: Compliance costs and restrictions on data usage
- **GST on Coins**: Unclear tax treatment of coin issuance and redemption
- **Foreign Investment**: Limits on FDI in multi-brand retail
- **Travel Regulations**: Hotel booking regulations vary by state

### Competition

- **PhonePe/Paytm IPO**: Large funded competitors could double down on merchant loyalty
- **Swiggy/Zomato**: Existing aggregator dominance in food delivery
- **Google Pay**: Tez/Google Pay could launch a loyalty layer
- **Amazon Pay**: E-commerce giant with loyalty program
- **New Entrants**: Any well-funded startup could clone the model

### Technical

- **Microservices Reliability**: 18 backend services must all be healthy for platform to work
- **Coin Accounting Errors**: Any bug in coin issuance/redemption could cause financial loss
- **Security Breaches**: Payment data, wallet balances are high-value targets
- **Scalability**: MongoDB/Redis must handle 10x traffic growth
- **BullMQ Queue Backlog**: Worker failures could delay notifications and settlements

### Financial

- **Negative Unit Economics**: Early stage may require subsidizing merchants/consumers
- **Coin Liability**: Total outstanding coins = contingent liability on balance sheet
- **Merchant Churn**: If merchants don't see ROI, they will leave
- **Burn Rate**: Multiple Render services + Vercel + EAS = significant monthly spend
- **Currency Risk**: INR fluctuations impact international settlement
